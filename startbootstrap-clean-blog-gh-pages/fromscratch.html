Best Classic Books

By Season


The following is a list of classic books perfect for each season. Each book is either set in that season or fits perfectly into the season based on aesthetic.


Spring

  1. The Enchanted April - Elizabeth von Arnim
    • A group of four English women escape their ordinary lives by renting a castle in Italy for the month of April. Surrounded by sunshine, beauty, and newfound freedom, they each undergo quiet personal transformations, rediscovering joy, love, and themselves.
  2. Emma - Jane Austen
    • A clever but sometimes misguided girl decides to play matchmaker, but despite her best efforts, it backfires terribly. As her plans unravel, she learns important lessons about humility, self-awareness, and the true nature of love.
  3. A Room With a View - E. M. Forster
    • A girl visits Italy for a summer and is torn between societal expectations and her desire for passion and authenticity. Through moments of beauty, conflict, and self-discovery, she learns to follow her heart rather than conform to convention.
  4. Wuthering Heights - Emily Brontë
    • This is a dark, passionate tale of love and revenge perfect for early spring. Set against the bleak, misty Yorkshire moors, the novel explores how obsession and vengeance can echo across generations.

Summer

  1. Pride and Prejudice - Jane Austen
    • A girl navigates issues of class, marriage, and morality in early 19th-century England, all while clashing with the reserved yet intriguing Mr. Darcy. Through wit and misunderstandings, the novel explores how first impressions can be misleading and how love grows through self-awareness and change.
  2. Crime and Punishment - Fyodor Dostoevsky
    • A troubled student commits murder, then struggles under the crusing weight of guilt and moral conflict. The oppresive heat of late summer fits the oppresiveness Raskolnikov feels as he grapples with his guilt.
  3. The Great Gatsby - F. Scott Fitzgerald
    • Set in the glittering 1920s, this book follows one man's obsessive pursuit of a girl. The novel emphasizes illusion, desire, and the elusive nature of the American Dream.
  4. Tuck Everlasting - Natalie Babbitt
    • A young girl discovers a family who achieved immortality after drinking from a spring. She must decide whether to embrace eternal life or accept change and mortality.

Fall

  1. Frankenstein - Mary Shelley
    • A scientist creates life from death, only to be horrified by his creation.
  2. The Bell Jar - Sylvia Plath
    • A promising young woman's life begins to unravel under the pressure placed on her by society. As she recovers, she explores her identity, expectations, and finds clarity within herself.

Winter

  1. Little Women - Louisa May Alcott
    • Four sisters grow up navigating family, love, ambition, and loss during and after the Civil War. This book shows everyday joy and struggles and how to make each day special.
  2. The Giver - Lois Lowry
    • In a carefully controlled society where pain, memory, and strong emotion have been eliminated, a boy is chosen to inherit the community's past. He discovers both the beauty and the cost of a life without true choice.
